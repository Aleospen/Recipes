Ramen is one of those foods that has taken on a life of its own. While many don't know how ramen became such an instant hit with anime fans outside of Japan, it may be due to the popular long-running series Naruto. Ramen was not only seen as food, but also a symbol of friendship, family, and happiness. Japanese ramen is made of broth, noodles, and different toppings. If there's one anime food that any foodie must try, it's ramen!

Recipe

  1. 2 tablespoons toasted sesame oil
  2. 4 garlic cloves, minced
  3. 1 2-inch piece ginger, minced
  4. 1 shallot, minced
  5. 1/4 cup miso paste
  6. 2 tablespoons toasted sesame seeds, coarsely ground with a mortar and pestle or spice grinder
  7. 8 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  8. Salt, to taste (if needed)
  9. Sugar, to taste (if needed)
  10. 4 ounce packages fresh ramen noodles
  11. 4 soft-boiled eggs
  12. 4 baby bok choy, quartered and blanched (or substitute about 12 ounces other veggies)
  13. 1 bunch scallions, thinly sliced
  14. La-yu (Japanese chili oil), to serve (optional)

Cooking

  1. In a large wok or Dutch oven over medium heat, heat sesame oil until fragrant.
  2. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and shallots. Stir-fry until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Add the miso paste and ground sesame seeds and stir to incorporate.
  4. Add the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and/or sugar, if needed.
  5. Meanwhile, bring a large pot of unsalted water to a boil over high heat and cook the ramen noodles according to package directions.
  6. Divide ramen noodles between 4 bowls. Ladle soup base over cooked noodles.
  7. Halve soft-boiled eggs and add to ramen bowls.
  8. Garnish with bok choy, sliced scallions, and la-yu.

Enjoy your ramen!
